# Thinking Before Building

> A deliberate practice of staying in the problem space longer — separating product thinking from engineering thinking before any architecture is drawn.

**Right now:** Explore frameworks and mental models that help founders and engineers stay in the problem space longer before converging on implementation.

**Last insight:** The impulse to design systems is so strong that it crowds out the harder work of understanding what the system is actually for.

---

## Why this matters

Software engineers are trained to solve problems by building. The moment a problem is described, the mind reaches for architecture, data models, and implementation choices. But this is often the wrong move — and the instinct itself is the bug.

The "what" and "why" shape everything downstream. Getting them right is harder and slower than writing code, and therefore easier to skip. This idea is about building the discipline not to skip it — as a personal skill and as a framework worth sharing.
