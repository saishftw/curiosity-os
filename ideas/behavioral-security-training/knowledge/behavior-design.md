# Behavior design

The core bet: behavior changes at the **moment of action**, not at the moment of
instruction.

> **Reframe:** Stop asking "do they know the policy?" Start asking "what happens in
> the half-second before a risky click?"

## A useful model

Fogg's model: `Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt`. Training only touches
motivation, and only briefly. The unused levers are the interesting ones:

- **Ability** — make the safe path the easy path.
- **Prompt** — a cue delivered exactly at the moment of risk.

## Micro-moment intervention

A tiny, well-timed interruption right before a risky action:

1. Detect the risky moment (unfamiliar sender, credential page off-domain).
2. Interrupt gently — one specific, human line.
3. Make the safe choice the default.
